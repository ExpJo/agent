## Language & culture

### Bill 96 / French language (high confidence)

Final major provisions effective **June 1, 2025**:

- Enterprises with **25+ employees** (6+ months) must register with OQLF and francize
- Consumer contracts, warranties, product labeling require French at equal or greater prominence
- Fines: $3,000–$30,000+ for violations

Sources: [Éducaloi](https://educaloi.qc.ca/en/capsules/francization-rules-for-employers/), [Norton Rose Fulbright](https://www.nortonrosefulbright.com/en/knowledge/publications/ea754c00/reform-of-the-charter-of-the-french-language-changes-effective-as-of-june-1)

**Opportunity patterns:**
- Compliance checklists, employee communication templates, audit prep tools
- Bilingual SaaS where English competitors treat French as afterthought
- Training/coordination tools for francization committees

**Product requirement:** B2B tools sold to Quebec SMBs should default to **French-first UX** with English option.

### Bilingual market dynamics

Montreal is not "French OR English" — it's **both**, unevenly by borough and industry:

| Segment | Language tendency | GTM note |
|---------|-------------------|----------|
| Plateau / Mile End creatives | Mixed; English-friendly | Meetups often English |
| East End / Rive-Sud manufacturing | French-primary | French sales required |
| Downtown corporate / tech | Bilingual | English UI often OK |
| Government-adjacent vendors | French-required | Bill 96 critical |
| Tourism/hospitality | Bilingual essential | Seasonal staffing |

**Anti-pattern:** English-only product marketed as "for Montreal" without French support.

## Regulatory & policy tailwinds

### Short-term rentals (STR)

Montreal tightened rules: principal-residence STR limited largely to **peak tourist season** (~June–Sept); many listings non-compliant.

Source: [Canadian Press — STR bylaw](https://www.thecanadianpressnews.ca/quebec/despite-pushback-montreal-forges-ahead-with-strict-new-short-term-rental-rules/article_589ff38f-cd8c-5cbf-9fb8-1fffd4c76867.html)

**Opportunity patterns:** Permit tracking, compliance calendars, host tooling for legal seasonal rental  
**Risk:** Platform politics (Airbnb); regulatory changes

### Commercial leases (medium confidence pain, low confidence regulation)

Multiple 2025–2026 closures (Verdun Station W, Monk Blvd merchants) driven by rent hikes. Quebec **does not** regulate commercial rents like residential.

Sources: [Montreal Gazette](https://montrealgazette.com/news/verdun-station-w-closing-rent-hike/), [CBC](https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/montreal/ville-emard-commercial-rent-increases-1.7490193)

**Opportunity patterns:**
- Rent comp databases by SDC corridor
- Lease renewal reminder + negotiation prep tools
- `[hypothesis]` Standard commercial lease templates if QS policy advances

### Environmental / GES

Quebec **Plan for a Green Economy 2030** drives programs like INNOV-R PME (GES reduction R&D). More relevant to funded R&D than 7-day MVPs, but signals **corporate reporting demand**.

## Business culture

### Quebec entrepreneurship norms

- **Relationship-driven B2B:** Cold email works but warm intros via PME MTL, YES, SDC faster
- **Modest risk tolerance:** Bootstrapping respected; VC not required for success narrative
- **Local preference:** "Produit québécois" positioning wins in home services (see Aidiway vs US platforms)
- **Government as customer/partner:** Municipal and provincial procurement favors local, French-capable vendors

### PME MTL network

Six borough-sector PME MTL centers offer **free advisory**. Ideas that align with their mandates (job creation, Montreal Island) get distribution help.

URL: https://pmemtl.com/en

## Seasonality

Montreal revenue curves are **not flat**. Score **Timing Catalyst** higher when launch aligns:

| Season | Demand spikes | Idea examples |
|--------|---------------|---------------|
| Winter (Nov–Mar) | Snow removal, heating, indoor events | Property manager winter ops |
| Spring (Apr–May) | Construction, moving, terraces prep | RBQ contractor scheduling |
| Summer (Jun–Aug) | Festivals, Grand Prix, STR, tourism | Event vendor logistics, host tools |
| Fall (Sep–Oct) | Back to school, Q4 B2B budgets | Training/compliance before year-end |
| Convention season | Palais des congrès events year-round; summer peak | Hospitality B2B tools |

**2025 data:** 477 business events, 1M+ visitors, **$438M** economic impact ([Newswire Jan 2026](https://www.newswire.ca/news-releases/business-tourism-2025-a-remarkable-performance-for-montreal-and-quebec-884981338.html)).

## Quick "Montreal specificity" test

Before collecting an idea, answer:

1. **Name a borough or SDC** where this idea lands first
2. **Name a French and English search term** customers would use
3. **Name a Montreal regulation, event, or competitor** that makes this timely
4. **Explain why Toronto launch wouldn't be identical**

If question 4 is hard, Montreal Anchor score ≤4.
